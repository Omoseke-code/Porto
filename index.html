About Me

Hi, my name is Salami Omoseke Halimah, I am a student of Agriculture in the University of Ilorin. I am from Nigeria. I am a dedicated learner or so does my school results say, top of my class at the university.

My life has taken a new direction into tech, planning on facing it full on. I got interested in starting a tech career because I had this enlightening conversation with a friend of mine, she’s a product manger currently interning at some company. She’s introduced me to the Tech4dev women techster’s bootcamp because that’s where she also started

My experience with tech has been pretty sweet and bitter at the same time. After the talk with my friend I came across an ad for a course with Udacity. I applied and chose Javascript, it was quite an exciting experience, it was a new thing, I flowed with the teachings until I came across object-oriented Javascript which was a few weeks after I started learning, things became a little complicated for me.

The bootcamp started and we kicked off with learning HTML and CSS of which I didn’t have any idea of. I got to learn about the basics of these new things in a week with tutorials here and there, reading articles, studying from when I woke up till midnight sometimes. I remember that one time I had this really bad headache I could barely do anything but oh well another day came and I continued learning.

I got excited through my first week in the bootcamp practiced with some type of tribute website, something I did on Fela. It was pretty rough though but got me excited. Learning bootstrap got me even more excited. Our first assessment was a form, I was really proud of myself for the outcome.

We’re in week two of the bootcamp, talking about Javascript. It hasn’t been easy but I’ll try as much as I can to continue in this path and keep improving.
